About Me

Hey there! My name is Keertana Namuduri and I’m a third-year student at UCLA, majoring in Cognitive Science and minoring in Digital Humanities. I’m a big picture thinker who loves to learn about a wide variety of topics and connect the dots between them. I have experience in UX, marketing, computer science and business development through internships and coursework, and am interested in pursuing a career in product management.

I’m passionate about using my skills to deliver products that have a social impact, specifically in health and wellness. I love spending the time to understand users’ needs and the psychology behind their motives to piece together the best marketing and development strategy. I’m driven by my desire to help others feel good through the work that I do, whether that be mentally, emotionally or physically.

My passion for mental health led me to join Wazo Connect, a peer mental health mentorship club, where I went through trainings to become a mental health peer mentor and joined the board as part of the Finance Committee and eventually, Director of Finance to help finance bi-weekly Wellness sessions for the UCLA community. My interest in making computer science inclusive led me to become an instructor, and eventually the Special Events Director and Social Chair, for TeachLA ACM, where I go to Title I schools in LAUSD and teach Python to students. Additionally, I participate in research in clinical psychology with the ChildFIRST Lab, while being an active member of the Cognitive Science Student Association’s Conference Committee and UniCamp.

This past summer, I worked with a team of 5 to create a digital organization tool inspired by bullet journaling, giving me insight into and experience with the product development cycle, from ideation to MVP. I’ve also participated in Adobe’s Creative Jams to practice my UI Design skills.

Outside of school, I love to spend my time finding new musicals to listen to, cross stitching, putting together jigsaw puzzles, or practicing yoga!

I’m always looking for opportunities to grow, personally and professionally, so feel free to reach out about anything!
